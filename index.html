FAQs

Frequently asked questions.

Quick answers to the most common questions about COAir — how it works, who it's for, and how we keep your data safe.

COAir is a private AI assistant purpose-built to live inside your enterprise data. Upload contracts, emails, spreadsheets, daily reports, and policies — then ask anything in plain language and get precise, sourced answers in under two seconds. Think of it as a senior analyst that has read every file your organisation has produced and is on call 24/7.
Three things set us apart:
  • Grounded in your data only. COAir answers exclusively from your indexed documents — no internet calls, no public model leakage.
  • Hybrid retrieval engine. Combines semantic vector search with structured SQL, so it understands meaning while still returning precise rows and figures.
  • Every answer is cited. Each response links back to the original file, page, paragraph, or cell — no black-box guessing.
Yes. Your data never leaves your environment. COAir can be deployed on your own infrastructure or in a fully isolated cloud tenant. Zero training on your data — ever. All content is encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3). Role-based access controls ensure each user only sees the documents their permissions allow.
Always. Every answer COAir produces includes citations linking back to the original source — with page numbers, paragraph anchors, spreadsheet cells, or email threads. Click a citation and the source document opens at the exact location. If COAir can't find supporting evidence in your data, it tells you so rather than guessing.
Yes. COAir parses .xlsx, .xls, .csv. It reads sheet structure, named ranges, formulas, and pivot tables. Typical questions include:
  • "Compare Q1 actuals vs forecast and flag variances over 10%."
  • "Which subcontractors have overdue items on the delay tracker?"
  • "Sum all approved variations from the cost report by trade."
Multi-sheet, multi-file aggregation works the same way as single-cell lookups.
Three structural guardrails:
  • Scope locked to your data. COAir physically cannot answer from anything outside your indexed sources.
  • Citations on every response. One click to verify the source — you stay in control of the truth.
  • "no relevant evidence found" is a valid answer. If no supporting evidence exists, COAir tells you. It will not fabricate a plausible-sounding response.
"Hallucination" is when a generative AI produces plausible-sounding but false information — it fills knowledge gaps with statistical guesses rather than facts. It's the single biggest reason enterprises hesitate to adopt AI.

COAir is built to be hallucination-resistant: it retrieves grounded passages from your data first, then generates strictly from that retrieved context. If the answer isn't supported by your sources, you'll see "no relevant evidence found" rather than a confident fabrication.
Yes. Give COAir the context — "draft a response to this client email about the delayed shipment" — and it will produce a reply grounded in your prior correspondence, the project file, contractual obligations, and any relevant policy. You stay in the loop: review the draft, edit, and send. COAir also handles formal letters, contract notices, board memos, and meeting summaries in the same way.
Yes — and this is one of the highest-value use cases for legal, claims, and dispute teams. Point COAir at a project, claim, or matter and ask it to build a chronology. It scans emails, meeting notes, daily reports, contract amendments, and correspondence to produce a date-ordered narrative with a source citation for every entry. Ideal for delay analysis, claim preparation, witness statement bundles, and disclosure review.
Yes. COAir can monitor your indexed data for early warning signals and surface them proactively:
  • Contract renewals and notice periods approaching
  • SLA breaches, missed milestones, and overdue actions
  • Budget overruns and cost variances beyond tolerance
  • Recurring incident patterns across daily reports
  • Unusual correspondence patterns (escalations, dispute language)
